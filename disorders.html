MATERNAL Disorders

Learn about common pregnancy and postpartum conditions.

What is Mental Health?

Mental health is an important part of overall well-being. It affects how we think, feel, behave, and cope with stress. Good mental health helps people face everyday challenges, build healthy relationships, make positive decisions, and enjoy a healthy and productive life. For mothers, maintaining good mental health during pregnancy and after childbirth is essential because it supports both their well-being and their baby's healthy growth and development.



What Causes Stress and Anxiety During Pregnancy?

Stress and anxiety during pregnancy are common because of the many physical, emotional, and lifestyle changes that occur during this period. While occasional stress is normal, prolonged stress and anxiety can negatively affect both the mother and the baby.

Common Causes

  • Hormonal changes
  • Physical discomfort
  • Fear of childbirth
  • Concerns about the baby's health
  • Financial concerns


How Can It Affect the Mother and the Baby?

Long-term stress and anxiety during pregnancy may have serious effects on both the mother and her baby.

Effects on the Mother

  • Poor sleep
  • Fatigue
  • High blood pressure
  • Increased risk of anxiety
  • Increased risk of depression
Effects on the Baby

  • Premature birth
  • Low birth weight
  • Delayed growth and development
  • Behavioral and emotional challenges later in life

Taking care of a mother's mental health helps improve pregnancy outcomes and promotes the baby's healthy development.



Common Maternal Disorders

Pregnancy and childbirth can bring different physical and emotional health conditions. Knowing the signs and symptoms allows mothers and families to seek medical care early and reduce health risks.


Anemia

Signs & Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Weakness
  • Pale skin
  • Dizziness
  • Shortness of breath

Management

  • Eat iron-rich foods
  • Take iron supplements
  • Regular blood check-ups
Gestational Hypertension / Preeclampsia

Signs & Symptoms

  • High blood pressure
  • Severe headache
  • Swollen hands, feet, or face
  • Blurred vision
  • Upper abdominal pain

Management

  • Monitor blood pressure regularly
  • Reduce stress and rest well
  • Take prescribed medicines
  • Attend prenatal check-ups
Gestational Diabetes

Signs & Symptoms

  • Excessive thirst
  • Frequent urination
  • Tiredness
  • Blurred vision
  • Increased hunger

Management

  • Follow a healthy diet
  • Exercise regularly
  • Monitor blood sugar
  • Take insulin or medicine if prescribed
Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

Signs & Symptoms

  • Pain during urination
  • Frequent urge to urinate
  • Lower abdominal pain
  • Cloudy or foul-smelling urine
  • Fever

Management

  • Drink plenty of water
  • Practice proper hygiene
  • Take antibiotics as prescribed
  • Avoid holding urine for too long
Postpartum Depression

Signs & Symptoms

  • Persistent sadness
  • Frequent crying
  • Anxiety
  • Trouble sleeping
  • Loss of interest in daily activities
  • Difficulty bonding with the baby

Management

  • Seek emotional support
  • Get enough rest
  • Counseling or therapy
  • Seek professional help if symptoms become severe
Hemorrhage

Signs & Symptoms

  • Heavy bleeding
  • Weakness
  • Dizziness
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Pale skin

Management

  • Seek emergency medical care immediately
  • Receive IV fluids or blood transfusion if needed
  • Close monitoring by healthcare providers
Mastitis

Signs & Symptoms

  • Breast pain
  • Swelling
  • Redness
  • Warm breast area
  • Fever

Management

  • Continue breastfeeding or pumping
  • Apply a warm compress
  • Rest well
  • Take antibiotics if prescribed
Hyperemesis Gravidarum

Signs & Symptoms

  • Severe nausea
  • Frequent vomiting
  • Dehydration
  • Weight loss
  • Weakness

Management

  • Drink fluids to prevent dehydration
  • Eat small, frequent meals
  • Take anti-nausea medicine if prescribed
  • Hospital care if severe
Postpartum Infections

Signs & Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Pelvic or abdominal pain
  • Foul-smelling discharge
  • Chills
  • Weakness

Management

  • Take antibiotics
  • Practice proper hygiene
  • Get enough rest
  • Consult a doctor immediately
